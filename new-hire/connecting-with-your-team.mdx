One of the most important parts of starting a new job is getting to know the people around you. Camino makes that easier by bringing your team introductions, contact information, and scheduled connections right into your onboarding portal — so you can start building relationships before you've even found where the coffee is.

<Note>
  Your HR team controls which people are introduced to you and when those introductions appear. Some profiles will be visible from day one, while others are scheduled to surface later in your onboarding — this is intentional and designed to pace your introductions thoughtfully.
</Note>

## The People Section of Your Portal

Navigate to the **People** tab in your portal to find everyone you'll be introduced to during onboarding. You'll see profile cards for your manager, your onboarding buddy, and your immediate teammates. Each card includes the person's name, role, photo, and a quick way to get in touch.

## Your Manager

Your manager's profile is one of the first things you'll see in the People section. Their card includes:

- **Role and department** — so you understand where they sit in the organization.
- **Contact information** — a link to their email address and, if your company uses it, their Slack handle.
- **Scheduled 1:1s** — some portals will surface your first 1:1 meeting details here, including date, time, and any agenda notes your manager has shared.

If you have questions before your first official meeting, your manager's contact card gives you everything you need to reach out.

## Your Onboarding Buddy

Many companies assign a dedicated onboarding buddy — a current employee who volunteers to help you get settled. Your buddy isn't your manager and isn't there to evaluate you; they're simply someone who remembers what it felt like to be new and wants to help you find your footing.

Your buddy's profile card works the same way as your manager's: you'll see their role, a short bio, fun facts they've shared, and direct contact links.

<Tip>
  Don't wait for your buddy to reach out first — send them a message on your first day! A simple "Hey, I'm excited to get started and looking forward to connecting" goes a long way. Buddies love hearing from new hires, and getting in touch early makes your first week feel a lot less overwhelming.
</Tip>

## Team Introductions

Beyond your manager and buddy, you'll find profile cards for your broader team. Each teammate's card typically includes:

  <Card title="Role & Responsibilities" icon="briefcase">
    A brief description of what your teammate works on, so you have context for who to go to with different types of questions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Fun Facts" icon="star">
    Personal tidbits your teammates have chosen to share — hobbies, favorite books, go-to lunch orders. These are great conversation starters.
  </Card>
  <Card title="How to Reach Them" icon="envelope">
    Direct links to email your colleague or message them on Slack (if your company uses it), right from their profile card.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Availability Notes" icon="clock">
    Some teammates include their time zone or working hours so you know the best time to connect.
  </Card>

## Scheduled Introductions

Not all team introductions appear on day one. Your HR team may have staged certain profiles to appear at specific points in your onboarding — for example, you might meet your direct teammates on Day 1, your wider department on Day 7, and cross-functional partners at the end of week two. This pacing prevents information overload and makes each introduction feel intentional rather than like a wall of names.

When a new introduction becomes available, you may see a notification in your portal or a new card appearing in the People section. Keep an eye on it throughout your first few weeks.

## Reaching Out to Teammates

Every profile card in the People section includes contact links — just click the email or Slack icon on a card to start a conversation. You don't need to wait for a formal introduction meeting to say hello; a quick message expressing excitement about joining the team is always welcome.

If your company uses a communication tool that isn't linked directly in the portal, your HR team will typically include setup instructions in your task list or resource library.

## The Company Directory

Once you're settled in with your immediate team, you can explore beyond it using the **Company Directory**. The directory lets you search for anyone at the company by name, role, or department — useful when you need to connect with someone outside your team or just want to know who's who. Access the directory from the People tab or the navigation menu, depending on how your portal is configured.
