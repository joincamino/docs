Your task list is the engine of your onboarding experience. It's where you'll find everything you need to do, organized into clear phases so you always know what to focus on right now and what's coming up next. Tasks can include anything from signing documents and setting up your laptop to scheduling a coffee chat with a teammate.

## Finding Your Task List

Navigate to the **Tasks** tab in your portal's main navigation to open your full task list. Tasks are grouped by phase, with the current active phase displayed at the top. You can scroll down to preview upcoming phases, though those tasks won't be actionable until the current phase is complete.

## Task Categories and Phases

Your onboarding is broken into phases that mirror your journey as a new hire. Common phases include:

- **Preboarding** — Tasks you can complete before your first day, such as paperwork or setting up accounts.
- **Week 1** — Foundational tasks to complete in your first week, like attending orientation or meeting your buddy.
- **First 30 Days** — Deeper dives into your role, team, and company culture.
- **Beyond** — Longer-horizon tasks that carry you through your first 60 or 90 days.

Your company may have customized these phases to fit their onboarding program, so the names or number of phases may differ slightly.

## Understanding Task Cards

Each task in your list is displayed as a card. Here's what you'll find on a task card:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Title & Description" icon="file-lines">
    A clear name for the task and a short summary of what it involves, so you know what to expect before you open it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Due Date" icon="calendar">
    When the task should be completed. Tasks approaching their due date will show a visual indicator to help you prioritize.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Instructions" icon="list-check">
    Step-by-step guidance for completing the task, written by your HR team. For tasks that involve external tools, you'll find links or prompts here.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Attachments" icon="paperclip">
    Any documents, forms, or files your HR team has attached for you to review, download, or submit as part of the task.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Marking a Task Complete

When you've finished a task, open the task card and click the **Mark as Complete** button. Your progress indicator on the home page will update automatically. For some tasks, completion may be verified automatically when you submit a form or connect a tool — in those cases, the task will mark itself complete once you're done.

<Note>
  Some tasks are marked **Required** and must be completed before your onboarding phase can close. Optional tasks are clearly labeled and won't block your progress if you skip them.
</Note>

## Required vs. Optional Tasks

Required tasks have a badge on their card and must be completed to unlock the next phase. Optional tasks are there to enrich your onboarding — things like watching a culture video or joining a Slack channel — but skipping them won't hold you back. If you're unsure whether a task is required, check the badge or open the task card for details.

## Tasks Assigned to Your Manager

Some tasks in your portal are assigned to your manager rather than to you. These might include things like scheduling your first 1:1 or completing a new hire check-in on their end. You can see these tasks for visibility — so you know they're happening — but you won't be able to mark them complete yourself. If you notice a manager-assigned task that seems overdue, it's worth giving your manager a friendly nudge.

## Progressing Through Phases

When you complete all required tasks in a phase, the next phase automatically unlocks and becomes available in your task list. You'll see a confirmation message celebrating your progress before the new phase opens. Optional tasks from a completed phase remain accessible even after the next phase unlocks, so you can always go back and finish them.

<Tip>
  You don't have to complete every optional task before moving on — but revisiting them when you have a quiet moment can be a great way to deepen your understanding of the company during your first few weeks.
</Tip>

## Overdue Tasks

If a task passes its due date without being completed, it will be flagged as **Overdue** with a red indicator on the task card. Overdue tasks don't disappear — you can still open and complete them. Check in with your HR team or manager if you're unsure whether an overdue task is still relevant or if the deadline can be adjusted.

## Getting Help with a Task

If a task is unclear or you're stuck, the best first step is to read through the full instructions inside the task card — your HR team often includes helpful context there. If you still have questions, reach out to your HR contact directly. Most portals include a help link or contact email in the navigation or footer.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="What if I can't complete a task?">
  Life happens — if you're unable to complete a task due to a technical issue, a missing tool, or a scheduling conflict, reach out to your HR team as soon as possible. They can extend deadlines, reassign tasks, or provide an alternative. Don't wait until a task is overdue to flag it.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I complete tasks out of order?">
  Within an active phase, yes — you can work through tasks in any order that makes sense for you. However, tasks in future phases won't become available until the required tasks in your current phase are complete. Think of it less like a strict sequence and more like a flexible checklist within each phase.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Why can't I see some tasks yet?">
  Tasks in upcoming phases are locked until you've completed the required tasks in your current phase. This is intentional — it keeps your task list focused and prevents information overload. As you progress, new tasks will unlock automatically. If you believe a task is missing or was assigned in error, contact your HR team.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Who can see my task progress?">
  Your manager and your company's HR team can see which tasks you've completed and which are still open. This helps them support you proactively — for example, if a required task has been sitting incomplete for a while, they may reach out to see if you need help. Your task progress is not visible to other colleagues.
</Accordion>
