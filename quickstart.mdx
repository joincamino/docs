This guide walks you through everything you need to do to go from zero to a fully configured onboarding experience in Camino. By the end, you'll have a live onboarding template assigned to a new hire and a clear picture of what they'll see when they log in to their portal. No prior setup is required — just a work email address and about ten minutes.

<Steps>

  <Step title="Create Your Camino Account">
    Head to [app.joincamino.com](https://app.joincamino.com) and click **Sign Up**. Enter your work email address and choose a strong password, or sign up with Google using your work account for faster access. Once you verify your email, you'll be prompted to enter your **company name** — this becomes the name of your Camino workspace and will appear throughout the platform and in communications your new hires receive. Click **Create Workspace** to continue.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Up Your Workspace">
    Before you build anything, take a few minutes to make Camino feel like your own. From the **Settings** page, upload your **company logo** and select your **brand colors** — these appear in the new hire portal, so your employees see a cohesive, professional experience from day one. Next, head to **Team Members** and invite at least one colleague (such as a fellow People team member or an IT admin) so they can be assigned tasks or help manage onboarding alongside you.

    <Tip>
      You can always update your branding and team settings later. That said, setting them up now ensures that any preview you run in Step 5 reflects your real new hire experience.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create Your First Onboarding Template">
    Navigate to **Templates** in the left sidebar and click **New Template**. Give your template a descriptive name — for example, "Software Engineer Onboarding" or "Sales New Hire - US Remote." Then choose the **role or team** this template applies to, which helps you stay organized as your library grows.

    With your template open, start adding **tasks**. Each task has a title, an optional description, an assignee (the new hire, their manager, IT, or a custom stakeholder), and a due date relative to the hire's start date (for example, "Day 1," "Day 3," or "Day 30"). Add a mix of tasks that cover equipment setup, policy reading, team introductions, and any role-specific training your new hires need.

    You can also attach **resources** — links, documents, or videos — directly to tasks or to the template itself so new hires have everything they need in one place.

    <Tip>
      If your Camino plan includes the **Template Library**, check it before building from scratch. The library contains pre-built templates for common roles and industries that you can import and customize in minutes, saving you significant setup time.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Your First New Hire">
    Go to **New Hires** in the left sidebar and click **Add New Hire**. Fill in the following details:

    - **Full name** — how they'll be greeted in their portal
    - **Work email address** — where their portal invitation will be sent
    - **Role** — their job title or position
    - **Start date** — used to calculate all relative task due dates automatically

    Once you've entered their information, select the template you created in Step 3 from the **Assign Template** dropdown and click **Save**. Camino immediately generates a personalized onboarding plan for this hire, calculating every task due date from their start date and preparing their new hire portal.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Preview the New Hire Experience">
    Before your new hire receives their invitation, see exactly what they'll see. Open the new hire's record and click the **Preview Portal** button in the top-right corner. This opens a live, read-only view of their personalized portal — including their task list, attached resources, team introductions, and company branding.

    Use this preview to confirm that tasks are in a logical order, due dates look correct, and the content is clear and welcoming. If anything needs adjusting, close the preview, edit the template or the individual hire's plan, and preview again. When you're happy with the experience, click **Send Invitation** to deliver the portal link to your new hire's inbox.
  </Step>

</Steps>
